What Can You Do About This?
Although tech corporations go to great lengths to make users attached-- and even reliant on-- their software and hardware, there are many steps you can take to moderate or entirely cut off your tech dependence.

1. Traditional Screen Time Restrictions. Modern smartphones have inbuilt tools designed to help reduce screen time use. On an Apple device, you can set up screen time restrictions in settings.

2. Third Party Screen Time Restrictions. Apps like One Sec and Opal force-close selected apps to delay usage.

3. Grayscale Filter. Switching a smartphone display to black-and-white reduces the stimulation and visual interest of the phone and all apps on it, making it less addictive. On an Apple device, you can do this by going to settings > accessibility > display & text size > color filters.

4. Eliminating Mobile Social Media Access. All conventional social media platforms accessible by smartphone can also be used via a desktop or laptop computer. Deleting said social media app off of your phone means you can't compulsively and constantly use it, but you can still stay active on social media via computer.

5. Limiting Distractions on Computer. On a computer, most social media and otherwise distractions are accessed through the web browser. You can make these sites less distracting with web extensions (such as DistractionFree YT) or block them completely with tools like Leechblock.

6. Minimizing Smartphone Functionality. For most people, the only functions of a smartphone that are truly 'essential' are texting, calling, music, photos, internet search, and navigation. You can minimize your screen time by deleting all non-essential apps, like mobile games, streaming, dating apps and online betting. Fundamentally, most apps are designed to hold your attention for as long as possible to get you to spend money or show you advertisements (although some are worse than others.)

7. Minimizing Smartphone UI. Beyond deleting non-essential apps, your smartphone home screen can be stripped of stimulating elements by getting rid of all other apps from immediate access. While keeping main functions like calling or texting on your homepage, hide less useful items (e.g. banking app or taxi service) from view, with the ultimate goal of making your smartphone as boring as possible to discourage use.

8. Deleting Social Media Accounts. This wipes all data that large software corporations have collected on you and stops them from keeping you hooked.

9. 'Dumb Phone' use. Although you can do a lot to keep a smartphone from being distracting, using an intentionally less functional phone only allows the bare essentials. In the modern day, using a dumb phone is the most grounded way to exist in society without extreme personal inconvenience. To switch to a dumb phone, first pick which one you want. Phones like the TCL Flip or Cat S22 Flip are fairly affordable, or use sites like Dumb Phone Finder to find another phone that meets your criteria (important to note is that some dumb phones can download apps, like navigation or music streaming, while others only allow you to use pre-downloaded apps). Make sure that whatever dumb phone you get is supported by your cell service provider. Once you have your dumb phone, switch your SIM card from your smartphone to your dumb phone (if you have an eSIM, you may have to call your cell service provider to have them reissue a physical sim card).

As mentioned, some dumb phones have the ability to download streaming apps, which allow you to listen to music as you might on a smartphone. Additionally, almost all dumb phones have built-in mp3 players, which do not allow for live music streaming but do open up possibilities for listening to downloaded music unavailable on streaming platforms. Digital downloads of songs can be purchased online, and there are additionally tools that convert music from streaming services to mp3 downloads (please note that The Luddite Club does not encourage piracy. Please obtain all downloads legally.)

There are several ways to get around with a dumb phone. Some dumb phones support navigation apps like Google Maps, while others require printed out or otherwise prepared directions. For taxi services, all dumb phones allow calls, which can either be directed to a local taxi company, or to Uber's calling service. The Uber hotline is 1-833-USE-UBER (1-833-873-8237), and, when called, will assign you a taxi without needing to use the Uber app. The Uber hotline operates from 4:00 AM to 10:00PM anywhere that Uber is available in the United States.
